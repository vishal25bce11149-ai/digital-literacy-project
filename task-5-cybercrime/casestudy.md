### Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud



###### UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a common type of cybercrime where scammers trick users into transferring money or revealing sensitive banking details. This crime usually happens through social engineering techniques.

###### 

###### Typically, the fraud begins when a scammer contacts the victim via phone call, SMS, or messaging apps, pretending to be a bank official, customer support agent, or even a buyer/seller. First, they create urgency or trust by offering rewards, resolving fake issues, or confirming a transaction. Next, they send a fake payment request or ask the victim to scan a QR code. The victim is misled into believing that scanning the code will receive money, but instead, it authorizes a payment. In some cases, victims are asked to share OTPs, UPI PINs, or click on phishing links that capture their details.

###### 

###### Students, elderly people, and individuals unfamiliar with digital payments are the most common targets, as they may lack awareness of such scams.

###### 

###### The consequences can be serious. Victims may lose significant amounts of money within minutes, and recovering the funds is often difficult. It can also lead to stress, loss of trust in digital systems, and potential misuse of personal data. This highlights the importance of digital literacy, awareness, and cautious online behavior while using UPI and other payment platforms.
